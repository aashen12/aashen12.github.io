Andy Shen

I am a fourth-year PhD Candidate in Statistics at UC Berkeley. I am advised by Haiyan Huang and Sam Pimentel. My research is supported by the NSF Graduate Research Fellowship.

My research is motivated by applied problems in health policy and medicine. I am interested in developing causal inference methods to help scientists make robust and transparent causal claims. I am also interested in solving data-driven problems using statistical learning and causal inference tools.

Much of my research is collaborative. I am grateful to be working alongside scientists and clinicians from Genentech, Kaiser Permanente, and the Children's Hospital of Philadelphia.

I graduated from UCLA in June 2021 with my B.S. in Statistics. I have spent many summers as a statistics intern at Los Alamos National Laboratory and most recently completed a biostatistics internship at Denali Therapeutics in summer 2023.

Feel free to connect with me via email or LinkedIn.

Teaching
Statistics 230A: Linear Models (Spring 2025)
Graduate Student Instructor (GSI), UC Berkeley

Instructor: Sam Pimentel

Master's level core course covering theory of least-squares estimation, interval estimation, and tests under the general linear fixed effects model with normally distributed errors. Large sample theory for non-normal linear models. Two and higher way layouts, residual analysis. Effects of departures from the underlying assumptions. Robust alternatives to least squares.
